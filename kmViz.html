The k-Means algorithm

During my study of data science and machine learning, I came across a large number of algorithms, some of which impressed my with their simplicity and effectiveness.

One of those was the k-means clustering algorithm, an unsupervised learning algorithm, and in particular its implementation by Stuart P. Lloyd, which is also known as the Voronoi iteration.

A k-means algorithm's objective is to, given a certain number n, of data points, or observations, assign them to k different clusters.
